Hospitality · The Anantha, Goa · 2025

A lobby that behaves like a coastline at dusk.

The brief asked for "five-star arrival." We answered with restraint — travertine underfoot, walnut at the eye-line, and a single brass datum line that follows you from the door to the lounge.

Turnkey delivery meant we controlled every layer, from the slab to the switch plates. That control is the whole argument: a room reads as calm only when nothing in it is accidental.

Daylight was the first material. We modelled the western glazing through an entire afternoon before a single finish was specified, then chose stones for how they hold warmth as the sun drops.

The result is an interior that changes character three times a day — bright and architectural at noon, golden and intimate by seven.

"We don't decorate rooms. We decide how they will feel at every hour."— Studio Principal, VELMONT
